# ESG Adoption and Cost of Capital in Family vs. Non-Family Firms: Evidence from Bangladesh

**Authors:** Siam Mahmud¹*, Nawshin Nawal Khan¹  
**Affiliation:** Department of Economics and Social Sciences, BRAC University, Dhaka, Bangladesh  
**Presented at:** RUEC 1st International Research Conference, Rajshahi, Bangladesh (September 6–7, 2025)  
**Contact:** siaamsaadman@gmail.com

## Research question

How do ESG practices influence borrowing costs differently for family vs. non-family firms in Bangladesh?

---

## Background

Bangladesh's corporate lending rates rose from 11.52% (June 2024) to 12.11% (May 2025), making cost of capital a critical concern for firms. Around 70% of established industries are family-owned, creating a structurally distinct corporate landscape. Despite Bangladesh Bank's green finance programs offering concessional loans at ≤5%, uptake remains low due to information gaps and weak ESG standardization.

This study asks whether ESG disclosure can reduce financing costs — and whether that effect differs between family and non-family firms.

---

## Data and methodology

- **Sample:** Publicly listed firms on the Dhaka Stock Exchange (DSE), 2015–2024
- **ESG scores:** Constructed from annual reports, sustainability reports, and third-party ratings
- **Firm classification:** Family vs. non-family based on controlling ownership, management roles, and succession structure
- **Dependent variable:** Cost of capital (WACC, borrowing rates, implied cost of equity)
- **Independent variables:** ESG score (disaggregated into E, S, G components), family firm dummy, ESG × family firm interaction
- **Controls:** Firm size, age, leverage, industry, ownership structure

**Regression model:**

Cost_of_Capital(i,t) = β₀ + β₁ESG(i,t) + β₂FamilyFirm(i,t) + β₃(ESG × FamilyFirm)(i,t) + β₄Controls(i,t) + ε(i,t)

**Estimation methods:**
1. Fixed effects panel regression (controls for unobservable firm-specific factors)
2. Panel GMM — Generalized Method of Moments (addresses endogeneity)
3. Robustness checks using lagged ESG variables and sector-specific fixed effects

---

## Key findings

- **ESG reduces borrowing costs:** Firms with higher ESG scores show significantly lower borrowing rates, controlling for size, industry, and leverage
- **Family vs. non-family gap:** ESG-driven cost reduction is stronger for non-family firms; family firms show smaller overall effects
- **Governance matters most for family firms:** Improvements specifically in the G (governance) component — board independence, transparency, succession planning — produce the most noticeable borrowing cost reductions for family-owned businesses
- **Case insight:** BRAC Bank, which dedicated 82% of its assets to sustainable finance in 2024, illustrates that strong ESG practices translate into better financing outcomes

---

## Policy implications

| Stakeholder | Recommendation |
|---|---|
| BSEC / Bangladesh Bank | Enforce standardized ESG reporting; incentivize adoption to reduce information asymmetry |
| Investors | Integrate ESG metrics into credit assessments; treat ESG disclosure as a proxy for lower default risk |
| Family firms | Invest in governance transparency — board independence and succession planning — to access cheaper capital and attract global investors |
